## 3. Typography (slice 2 rendering contract for new content shapes)

This is the only section with genuinely new specification. Phase 02.2 shipped paragraphs (`<p>`), unordered lists (`<ul>` plain bullets), nested unordered lists (one level), and ordered lists (`<ol>` with multi-line items + nested `<ul>` inside one item). The Икономика chapter also used `<strong>` inline emphasis at the start of policy items.

Slice 2 introduces additional content shapes from the source PDF that did not appear in slice 1. The contract below specifies how each shape MUST render.

### 3.1 Inherited typography ramp

| Role | Mobile | Desktop | Weight | Where it appears in /agenda |
|------|--------|---------|--------|------------------------------|
| Page h1 (display) | 1.875rem (30px) | 2.5rem (40px) | 800 (Gilroy) | Once: "Програма" — outside the `<article>`, styled via `font-display text-3xl` (live page.tsx line 58). UNCHANGED. |
| Section h2 (chapter title) | prose-default | prose-default | prose-default | Each top-level coalition chapter. Styled by `prose-lg`. **Do not override with Gilroy.** prose default `<h2>` size at `prose-lg` ≈ 1.875rem; weight 800 by default. |
| Sub-section h3 (within a chapter) | prose-default | prose-default | prose-default | NEW shape in slice 2 — appears in chapters that have explicitly named sub-divisions in the source (e.g., "България е свободна и демократична държава", "Ценности и традиции", "България е християнска държава" inside "Защита на българските ценности..."; or "Съдебни разходи и правна помощ" / "Срокове" inside "Правосъдие"). Styled by `prose-lg` `<h3>` defaults. See §3.3.A. |
| Sub-sub-heading h4 | prose-default | prose-default | prose-default | NOT used. The source has at most h2/h3 hierarchy. | Body | 1rem (16px) | 1rem (16px) | 400 (Roboto) | All `<p>` content. prose-default at `prose-lg` ≈ 1.125rem with `line-height: 1.5`. |
| Inline emphasis | inherits | inherits | 600 (`<strong>`) / italic (`<em>`) | `<strong>` used as inline policy-name marker at the start of `<li>` items (Икономика precedent). `<em>` available but not expected for slice 2 content. See §3.3.D. |

### 3.2 Locked: do not override prose defaults

`prose prose-slate prose-lg max-w-none` is a load-bearing class set. **Do not** add inline classes that override font size, line height, font family, or color tokens of body elements. The walking skeleton verified the defaults look correct on real coalition content. The acceptable overrides are:
- Adding `id="…"` to `<h2>` elements (anchor targets)
- Adding `<section>` wrapping for semantic grouping (no styling effect)
- Adding `className="mt-2 mb-6 font-display text-3xl"` to the page-level `<h1>` (already in place; it's outside the `<article>`)

### 3.3 New content shape contracts (slice-2 specific)

#### 3.3.A — Sub-section headings (`<h3>`)

When a top-level chapter contains explicitly named sub-divisions in the source PDF (visually presented as paragraph-titled blocks, NOT as numbered list items), render each sub-division as an `<h3>` followed by its body.

**Apply to:**
- Inside "Защита на българските ценности, традиции и интереси" (line ~1247 onward): sub-divisions "България е свободна и демократична държава", "Ценности и традиции", "ЛГБТ и нахлуването на полова идентичност в обществото", "България е християнска държава", "България е европейска държава", and any further sub-divisions through end of chapter. Each = `<h3>{label}</h3>` (no anchor id; anchors are top-level only).
- Inside "Правосъдие" (line ~899): "Съдебни разходи и правна помощ" (line 1007), "Срокове" (line 1015) appear as inline sub-headings — render as `<h3>` followed by their body paragraphs.

**Do NOT apply to:**
- Numbered policy items in Образование ("1. Промяна на учебното съдържание", "2. Връщане на училището…") — these are `<li>` items inside an `<ol>` per §3.3.C, NOT `<h3>`s. The source's "1." prefix is the cue for ordered-list rendering.
- The Икономика chapter (already shipped as `<strong>`-prefixed `<li>` items in slice 1) — slice 1's pattern is the precedent.

**Anchor ids on `<h3>`:** none. TOC entries are exclusively for top-level chapters (L-11). Sub-section h3s do NOT get URL fragments. If a future requirement asks for deep-linking to a sub-section, that is out of scope for this slice.

#### 3.3.B — Unordered lists (`<ul>` + `<li>`)

The slice-1 precedent ships two `<ul>` patterns:
1. Flat 6-item state-responsibility list (in Десен консенсус and again in Икономика).
2. Nested 4-item bullet list inside an `<li>` (in Икономика item 4, "Привличане на чуждестранни инвестиции").

Slice 2 introduces a third pattern:

3. **Long flat policy list** — Земеделие (line 466 onward) and similar chapters present a long sequence of bullet-points where the source uses inconsistent indentation/spacing but each is a discrete policy. Render as a single flat `<ul>` with one `<li>` per bullet. **Do not** split into multiple `<ul>` blocks unless the source clearly intervenes with a paragraph or sub-heading between groups.

**Style:** prose default disc bullets. **Do NOT** introduce custom markers (no `list-disc list-inside`, no Bulgarian-numeral bullets, no em-dash bullets — even where the source PDF visually uses em-dashes as bullet markers, the structural answer is `<ul><li>` with prose's default disc).

**Exception — em-dash sub-bullets in Култура (line 1111+):** The Култура chapter uses `–` (en-dash) prefixes as sub-bullets within numbered policy items 6–10. Render these as a nested `<ul>` (prose-default disc) inside the parent `<li>`. **Strip the source's `–` character from the rendered text** (the dash was the PDF's bullet marker, not part of the prose). Same rule as the Икономика item-4 nested `<ul>` precedent in slice 1.

#### 3.3.C — Ordered lists (`<ol>` + `<li>`)

Slice 1 precedent: two `<ol>` blocks in Икономика. Re-numbered 1-N in rendered HTML even when the source PDF restarts at "1." multiple times (pdftotext page-break artifact).

**Slice 2 patterns:**

1. **Образование numbered policies (line 565 onward):** "1. Промяна на учебното съдържание", "2. Връщане на училището към основните му задачи", "3. Възстановяване и защита на авторитета на учителя" appear as numbered sub-policies under sub-section "1. Нашата цел в областта на училищното образование…" — render as `<ol>` with each item starting with a `<strong>{policy-name}.</strong>` inline (matching the Икономика slice-1 pattern). Then "1. Спиране на безконтролното разрастване…", "2. Диференцирано финансиране…", "3. Диференцирано отношение…", "4. Увеличаване на средствата за наука…", "5. Промени в регулациите…" appear as a separate `<ol>` under sub-section "В сферата на висшето образование…" — same `<strong>`-prefix rendering.

2. **Култура 10-mеасure list (line 1145 onward):** the chapter explicitly describes a "пакет от 10 основни мерки" and lists them 1-10 in source. Render as a single `<ol>` with 10 `<li>` items. Each item starts with `<strong>{measure-name}.</strong>` and contains the body (which in items 6-10 includes a nested `<ul>` of em-dash sub-bullets per §3.3.B exception).

3. **`<li>` items with multi-paragraph bodies:** when an `<li>` contains a topic sentence followed by additional paragraph-length elaboration, wrap each subsequent paragraph in `<p>` tags inside the `<li>` (Икономика slice-1 precedent for the second `<ol>`'s "Бюджетна политика" item). prose styles `<li> > <p>` correctly.

**Numbering policy:** ALWAYS use real `<ol>` semantics — never hand-code "1." / "2." / "3." prefixes as text. The browser renders the numerals via `prose`'s `<ol>` styling. This applies even when re-numbering (e.g., source restarts at "1." but rendered HTML must continue 1-2-3-...-10).

#### 3.3.D — Inline emphasis (`<strong>`, `<em>`)

- `<strong>`: used to bold a policy name at the start of an `<li>` (Икономика precedent: `<strong>Бюджетна политика — Дългове и дефицити.</strong>` followed by body). Continue this pattern in slice 2 for the new `<ol>` blocks (Образование, Култура).
- `<em>`: not expected in slice 2 source content. If a planner finds source italics (rare in pdftotext output), render as `<em>` and let `prose` style it.
- **No `<u>`, no `<mark>`, no inline color spans.** prose doesn't style them and the source doesn't use them.

#### 3.3.E — Block quotes / lead paragraphs

The source PDF visually presents the first paragraph of each chapter with a small left-indent (suggesting a blockquote or lead-paragraph styling). Slice 1 rendered these as plain `<p>` elements (no indent, no `<blockquote>`). Slice 2 follows the same rule:

- **Render lead paragraphs as plain `<p>`.** Do NOT use `<blockquote>`. Do NOT add `lead` class or any indent override.
- **Rationale:** the source's indent is a typesetting convention from the PDF, not a semantic block-quote. Treating it as a quote would imply attribution to an external source, which is wrong — the coalition is the author of the entire document.

**If a future audit identifies a true source-quoted passage** (an external person or document being quoted within the agenda), THEN render that passage as `<blockquote>` and let `prose` style it. None has been identified in lines 319-end so far.

#### 3.3.F — Source punctuation fidelity

Inherited from slice 1 (SUMMARY.md ambiguity #5):
- Em-dashes (`–`), curly quotes (`„…"`), and non-breaking spaces are preserved verbatim.
- pdftotext line-wrapping is de-wrapped: blank line in source = paragraph break; consecutive non-blank lines join into one `<p>`.
- pdftotext numbering artifacts (lists that restart at "1.") are corrected via real `<ol>` semantics.

### 4.3 Open question for the planner: fate of `agenda.draftAlert`

The `agenda.draftAlert` key is currently used in TWO places:
1. The visible `<Alert>` body (removed by §4.1)
2. The page's `generateMetadata().description` field (live page.tsx line 28: `description: t('draftAlert')`)

Use #2 means the OG/Twitter card description currently reads "Програмата е в процес на финализиране." — which becomes false once §4.1 removes the alert.

**Resolution options for the planner:**
- **Option A (preferred):** Replace `description: t('draftAlert')` with a freshly-defined `description: t('metadataDescription')` key, with copy along the lines of `"Какво защитаваме и каква промяна искаме за МСП сектора в България. Пълна политическа програма на коалиция Синя България."` Drop `agenda.draftAlert` from `bg.json` after the swap. (This mirrors the pattern already established by `faq.metadataDescription` at `bg.json:270`.)
- **Option B:** Repurpose `agenda.draftAlert` (rename in-place via key migration) — more typing churn for the planner, less clarity for future readers.

**This UI-SPEC recommends Option A.** Final decision is the planner's; whichever path is chosen, the OG description must be true after the slice ships (no false "in process of finalization" claim).

**Transliteration scheme (locked, matches slice 1 precedent):**
- Cyrillic → Latin per the standard Bulgarian streamlined transliteration:
  - `я → ya`, `ю → yu`, `й → y`, `ь → y`, `ъ → a`, `ц → ts`, `ч → ch`, `ш → sh`, `щ → sht`, `ж → zh`, `х → h`
  - All other letters → their phonetic Latin equivalent (а→a, б→b, в→v, г→g, д→d, е→e, з→z, и→i, к→k, л→l, м→m, н→n, о→o, п→p, р→r, с→s, т→t, у→u, ф→f)
- Lowercase only.
- Spaces → `-`. Multi-word chapter names compress (e.g., "Социална политика" → `sotsialna-politika`, NOT `sotsialna_politika` or `socialnapolitika`).
- Punctuation (commas, "и", "на") in long h2 strings is dropped from the anchor (e.g., "Защита на българските ценности, традиции и интереси" → `zashtita-tsennosti` — short stable form, NOT a 6-word anchor).
- TOC labels (column 3 above) are also short stable forms — they appear in the sticky TOC and in the mobile `<details>` block, so they need to fit without wrapping. Full chapter titles appear in the rendered `<h2>` only.

**This anchor scheme is locked.** The unit test (L-12) asserts these exact IDs are present after slice 2 ships. If the planner needs to deviate (e.g., the coalition redlines a chapter title), update the unit test in lockstep — but anchor renames are otherwise prohibited because they break URL stability for any external link to the agenda.
